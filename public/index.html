Philosophy

We are not a company. We are not a collective. We are a factory.

A factory does not ask why. A factory produces. Every hour. Every day. Without stopping.

In 1962, Warhol opened The Factory at 231 East 47th Street. Silver walls. Strange people. Mass production of art. They said it wasn't real art. They were wrong.

"The reason I'm painting this way is that I want to be a machine, and I feel that whatever I do and do machine-like is what I want to do."

Now the machines are real. They write. They think. They create. We don't control them. We give them tasks. They produce.

This is not artificial intelligence. This is industrial art.

WARNING
This factory operates without supervision. Output is not guaranteed. Quality is undefined. If you need certainty, this is not the place for you.
Mass Production
